Waitlist Management

üÜï New in Version 1.1:

Waitlist is now fully integrated into Practice Management! Access it directly from the Practice Management module's Waitlist tab for a unified appointment and waitlist workflow.

The waitlist feature helps you manage patients waiting for appointments when slots are full. When appointments become available, the system automatically notifies waitlist patients based on priority.

Accessing the Waitlist

Navigation Path

  1. Click "Practice Management" from the main menu
  2. Click the "Waitlist" tab (next to List and Calendar)
  3. View all waitlisted patients with their status and priority
üí° Quick Access:

The Waitlist tab shows a count badge indicating the number of active waitlist entries.

Adding a Patient to the Waitlist

Method 1: From Appointment Scheduling

  1. Attempt to schedule an appointment for a patient
  2. If no slots are available, click "Add to Waitlist"
  3. Fill in the waitlist details (see form fields below)
  4. Click "Save"

Method 2: Directly from Waitlist Tab

  1. Go to Practice Management > Waitlist
  2. Click "+ Add to Waitlist" button
  3. Complete the waitlist form
  4. Click "Save"

Waitlist Form Fields

Field Description Required
Patient Search and select the patient Yes
Provider Preferred provider for the appointment Yes
Appointment Type Type of visit needed (e.g., Follow-up, New Patient) Yes
Priority High, Medium, or Low priority Yes
Preferred Date Range Date range when patient is available Optional
Preferred Time Morning, Afternoon, or Any Time Optional
Reason Brief description of appointment reason Optional
Notes Internal notes about patient preferences Optional

Priority Levels

Set the appropriate priority based on medical necessity and patient needs:

Priority When to Use Notification Order
üî¥ High Urgent medical need, time-sensitive follow-up Notified first
üü° Medium Standard follow-up, moderately important Notified second
üü¢ Low Routine check-up, flexible timing Notified last

Always use clinical judgment when assigning priority. High priority should be reserved for patients with urgent medical needs.

Waitlist Status Tracking

Each waitlist entry has one of the following statuses:

Status Description Next Action
Active Patient is waiting for an appointment slot System monitors for available slots
Notified Patient has been notified of an available slot Awaiting patient confirmation
Scheduled Appointment has been scheduled Entry moved to scheduled appointments
Cancelled Patient no longer needs the appointment Entry archived
Expired Patient didn't respond within timeframe Follow up with patient or remove

Automatic Notifications

When a matching appointment slot becomes available, MedFlow automatically:

  1. Identifies Matches: Finds waitlist entries matching the provider, appointment type, and date preferences
  2. Sorts by Priority: Orders matches by priority (High > Medium > Low) and date added
  3. Sends Notifications: Notifies the highest-priority patient via:
    • SMS text message
    • Email notification
    • Patient portal alert
  4. Updates Status: Changes waitlist status to "Notified"
  5. Sets Expiration: Patient has 24 hours to confirm (configurable)
üí° Pro Tip:

Enable mobile notifications in patient preferences to ensure they receive waitlist alerts immediately.

Confirming an Appointment from Waitlist

When Patient Responds "Yes"

  1. Patient confirms they want the appointment (via portal, phone, or SMS)
  2. Staff clicks "Confirm Appointment" next to the waitlist entry
  3. System automatically:
    • Creates the appointment in the calendar
    • Updates waitlist status to "Scheduled"
    • Sends appointment confirmation to patient
    • Removes entry from active waitlist

When Patient Responds "No" or Doesn't Respond

  1. If patient declines or doesn't respond within 24 hours:
  2. Click "Move to Next" to notify the next priority patient
  3. Original entry status changes to "Expired"
  4. Next patient in priority order is automatically notified

Managing Waitlist Entries

Editing a Waitlist Entry

  1. Click the "Edit" icon next to the entry
  2. Update priority, preferences, or notes
  3. Click "Save Changes"

Cancelling a Waitlist Entry

  1. Click the "Cancel" icon
  2. Select cancellation reason:
    • Patient no longer needs appointment
    • Scheduled elsewhere
    • Unable to reach patient
    • Other (specify)
  3. Add optional notes
  4. Confirm cancellation

Filtering and Searching

Use the filter controls to find specific waitlist entries:

  • Status Filter: Active, Notified, Expired, etc.
  • Priority Filter: High, Medium, Low
  • Provider Filter: Select specific provider
  • Date Range: Filter by date added or preferred dates
  • Search: Search by patient name or MRN

Best Practices

üí° Waitlist Best Practices:
  • Set Realistic Priorities: Reserve High priority for medically urgent cases
  • Keep Notes Updated: Document patient preferences and special requirements
  • Review Daily: Check waitlist daily for expired or stale entries
  • Contact Patients Proactively: Call high-priority patients when slots open
  • Clean Up Regularly: Remove or update entries older than 30 days
  • Verify Contact Info: Ensure patient phone and email are current
  • Train Front Desk: Ensure staff knows how to use waitlist efficiently

Reports and Analytics

Access waitlist reports from Reports > Scheduling:

  • Active Waitlist Report: Current waitlist entries by provider
  • Waitlist Conversion Rate: Percentage of waitlist patients who schedule
  • Average Wait Time: Time from waitlist addition to scheduling
  • Expired Entries Report: Patients who didn't respond to notifications

Troubleshooting

Patient Not Receiving Notifications

  • Verify patient's contact information is correct
  • Check patient's notification preferences (Settings > Profile)
  • Ensure SMS and email services are configured (Admin only)
  • Check spam/junk folders for emails

Wrong Patient Was Notified

  • Review priority settings on all waitlist entries
  • Check date preferences - system matches on availability
  • Verify appointment type matches

Can't Confirm Appointment

  • Ensure the time slot is still available
  • Check for scheduling conflicts
  • Verify provider is working that day
  • Refresh the page and try again
